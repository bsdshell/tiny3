Although I am a sometimes artist and hi-fi speaker builder I am also a fully employed painter at UBC. I'm not rich, but I'm also not a fool either. Regardless of location $500 is really all a room should ever be frankly. I don't know whats happened to home owners these days but greed, games and more greed are an ever present danger when renting now. I am just a very friendly, normal person trying to save for a down payment on a house (not in Vancouver of course.)

I am a well traveled 40yo Caucasian Canadian. My typical routine consists of Tea in morning, getting to work by 700am, coming home around 400pm, relaxing with a good quality beer and doing some research or listening to music on my laptop. I'm not a loud party guy and I'm usually in bed by 10pm. I am absolutely social but don't pry too much into other peoples business as a rule. You should have your own life/goals/budget and not be dependent on your tenants to always entertain or loan you money. I'm a bit of a minimalist and don't need much space for food, half a cupboard and one shelf in the fridge is more than enough. I'm a good cook and enjoy pretty much every kind of food there is, but I don't smoke up the kitchen or leave dishes everywhere. I've lived with many types of people over the years and enjoy cultural diversity so you should as well.

OK so here's my deal:

What I want - room for $500 a month, half month Damage deposit, no obligatory time contract, just pay as you go.

What you get - Rent on time, every time, no problems, no drama, and a promise to give 30 days notice if things don't work out.


I am looking to stay one to two years in Vancouver before buying a property elsewhere. This is my singular focused goal. So as long as we are good people with some empathy, then there is no reason I would want to move before then. If for whatever reason you don't want me there then this is not a problem. Just allow me the same courtesy of notice.

If this sounds good to you and your capable of understanding my situation, but not wanting to exploit it, then please email with a name and preferably a picture as well as your own situation/preferences. The price is not negotiable, but I am absolutely open to differen't needs and ideas. I drive a nice cargo van so I don't mind picking things up or dropping them off and so on.

If anything, thanks for listening to this rant.

Mic
